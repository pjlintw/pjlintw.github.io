I'm a CS Ph.D. student at [Virginia Tech](https://cs.vt.edu/)'s Large Language Models (LLMs) Lab, advised by [Tu Vu](https://tuvllms.github.io/). My research focuses on **collaborative and communal machine learning**, aiming to efficiently and effectively develop all-rounded LLMs. I explore methods that blend a diverse set of skills, encompassing parameter-efficient fine-tuning, modular composition, and model merging.

Previously, I received a Master's at the [Language Science and Technology Department (LST)](https://www.uni-saarland.de/en/department/lst/research.html) of Saarland University, where I worked with [Dietrich Klakow](https://www.lsv.uni-saarland.de/people/dietrich-klakow/) and [Vera Demberg](https://www.uni-saarland.de/lehrstuhl/demberg/members/verademberg.html). Prior to that, I contributed to the development of NLP systems for historical archives with [Richard Tsai](https://scholar.google.com.tw/citations?user=iDz3gJ4AAAAJ&hl=zh-TW) and [Yuan-ju Liu](https://www.harvard-yenching.org/person/liu-yuan-ju/) at [Academia Sinica](https://www.sinica.edu.tw/en). I obtained my Bachelor‚Äôs in History and was selected as a Google CSRMP Fellow in 2023.

### Research

My research broadly revolves around efficient NLP and desensitization. Problems that frame my research goals include:

- Improving the reuse of fine-tuning artifacts ([Intermediate-task Transfer Learning](https://aclanthology.org/2024.acl-srw.24/)).

- Efficiently and effectively transferring advanced skills (e.g., multilingualism, instruction-following, long-context handling, reasoning) for robust generalization ([CaT](https://arxiv.org/abs/2307.00382), [In-Context Prompt Editing](https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.00895)).

- Developing methods to identify quality data mixtures for pretraining and post-training ([Modeling Orthographic Variation](https://aclanthology.org/2024.lrec-main.1006/), [Target-aware Language Modeling](https://arxiv.org/abs/2409.14705/), [Sample Size Determination](https://aclanthology.org/2023.findings-acl.419/)).

- Advancing our understanding of LLM behavior to develop actionable principle (e.g. the mechanisms of knoweldge transfer during finetuning phase).
